This is the future web home of Aaron Brooks, a systems biology graduate student in Seattle, WA. Here are a few of the projects I have been working on.

Redefining modular regulatory states

Controling when, where, and how much of a gene product to produce is paramount for life. The mechanisms underlying this process, however, can be highly complicated, entirely environment-dependent, and difficult to discern from data. We have developed a new perspective on genetic regulation that helps demystify this complexity.

Are microbes intelligent?

What is intelligence? Is it a property of the human brain or, perhaps more generally, a feature of any system with components that interact in particular ways? In this work we explore whether microbes fufill criteria for being called 'intelligent'. We suggest how studying properties of microbial intelligence may shed light on how our own brains work.

How do cells adapt to new environments?

How do single-celled organisms keep pace with environments that constantly change? Do cells adapat differently to environments that change sporadically compared to those that change on slower timescales? In this review we explore the molecular mechanisms that cells use to acheive adaptation. We consider how these mechanisms vary according to environment.
