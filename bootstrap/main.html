Redefining modular regulatory states

Regulating gene production is paramount for life. Mechanisms underlying this process, however, can be complicated, environment-dependent, and difficult to discern from data. We developed a new perspective that helps demystify this complexity.

Are microbes intelligent?

What is intelligence? Is it a property of the human brain or, perhaps more generally, a feature of any system with components that interact in particular ways? In this work we explore whether microbes fufill criteria for being called 'intelligent'. We suggest how studying properties of microbial intelligence may shed light on how our brains work.

How do cells adapt to new environments?

How do single-celled organisms keep pace with environments that constantly change? Do cells adapt differently to environments that change sporadically compared to those that change on slower timescales? In this review we explore the molecular mechanisms that cells use to acheive adaptation. We consider how these mechanisms vary according to environment.
